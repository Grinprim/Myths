Character Rules & Formulas

1. Maximum Health Calculation

Your maximum health scales strictly off your character level and your Body's Brawn modifier. The level progression table provides a set amount of base HP, and you add [Brawn Total × Level] onto that.

LevelBase HP AddedCumulative HP
LVL 1+66
LVL 2+28
LVL 3+412
LVL 4+214
LVL 5+216
LVL 6+420
LVL 7+222
LVL 8+224
LVL 9+428
LVL 10+230
LVL 11+232
LVL 12+436

Formula: Cumulative Level HP + (Brawn Total × Level)

2. Armor Defense Calculation

Your final armor defense represents your structural durability. It is calculated by adding your Body's Fortitude modifier to your equipped defensive items and any path-specific bonuses.

Armor = 4 + Fortitude Total + Armor Value + Shield Value + Cloak Value + Artifact Value + Path Bonuses

3. Mana Pool Scaling

Maximum Mana points correspond directly to your overall experience level plus any additional spell bonuses.

Max Mana = Level + Level/3 (rounded down) + Spellcasting Bonus Mana

4. Spellcasting Reference

Challenge Score: 6 + Spirit Total
Attack Bonus: Arcane Total
Damage Bonus: Arcane Total / 2, rounded up
Known Spell Cap: Scholarship Total + Bonus Spell Slots
Bonus Spell Slots: School traits or custom bonuses you add manually

5. Speed & Movement

Speed has a flat base of 5 tiles, plus any manual Speed Bonus you add (from traits, gear, or conditions). Each tile of Speed lets you move to one directly-adjacent tile; diagonal movement does not skip over tiles.

Speed = 5 + Speed Bonus (manual input)

Jump Distance: Half your Speed, rounded up
Rough Terrain: Each tile costs 2 Speed instead of 1
Dash: Range set by its source; ignores opportunity attacks and terrain movement penalties

6. Trait Points & Feat Points

Trait Points (TP) are the budget you spend unlocking traits on the Paths tab. Your total grows automatically with Level, and can be further increased with the manual Bonus TP slot or certain species/traits.

Total TP = (2 × Level) + Bonus TP (manual input) + Trait/Species Bonuses

Feat Points work differently on this sheet than a flat per-level grant: each time you reach a Path Breakpoint, you choose between applying that path's passive feature, or converting it into +1 Feat Point to raise one Attribute or Skill of your choice by 1. This choice can be changed at any time from the breakpoint panel.

7. Guard, Grace & Final Blows

Guard: Temporary HP. Always lost before Health. Cannot be healed once lost — only regained through traits or external effects.
Grace (max 6): Gain +1 on a failed roll. A success doesn't reset Grace, but delays the next gain by one fail. Spend ALL Grace at once to boost a roll. Resets at the start/end of combat or on Sleep.
Final Blows (max 4): At 0 Health you fall unconscious. While unconscious, only Final Blows can harm you — you can resist 4 before dying (Narrator may adjust).

8. Exhaustion

The Exhaustion tracker (max 6) caps out at the point this condition becomes fatal — going past it without sleeping is lethal. Each point stacks a penalty on every roll you make.

Roll Penalty = −2 × Exhaustion (stacking)

A full 8-hour Sleep removes 1 point of Exhaustion and fully restores Health and Mana.

9. Carry Weight & Encumbrance

Your maximum carry weight is derived from your Body's Might and Brawn totals, your character level, and any backpack bonus you enter on the Inventory tab. Current weight is the sum of every item's weight in your main inventory grid.

Max Carry Weight = Might Total + Brawn Total + Level + Backpack Bonus

Current WeightStatus
≤ 1× MaxUnencumbered
> 1× MaxEncumbered — Movement halved
> 2× MaxOver Encumbered — Movement halved, disadvantage on Body/Skill
> 3× MaxCrushed — Movement 0, disadvantage on all checks, 1 damage/10 min

100 coins of carried currency counts as 1 unit of weight.

10. Armor & Weapon Proficiencies

One piece of equipment per slot (Armor, Shield, Cloak/Artifact) unless a trait says otherwise. Armor proficiency is gated by your Fortitude score; weapon proficiency limits are gated by Might and Finesse.

Armor TypeDefense Value / Fortitude Required
Shield+1–2 / 1+ Fortitude
Light+1–2 / 2+ Fortitude
Medium+2–3 / 3+ Fortitude
Heavy+4–5 / 4+ Fortitude

You can wield as many weapons as you have arms, but without traits only 1 weapon is fully effective. Might and Finesse set your max investment in Melee, Ranged, and Finesse weapon proficiencies — multiple points may go into the same proficiency.

11. Core Resolution (2d6)

Most actions are resolved by rolling 2d6 + Modifiers, succeeding if the total meets or beats the target's Challenge Score (CS).

Advantage: Roll one extra d6 (3d6 total), keep all — no upper limit on stacking.
Disadvantage: Remove one d6 (down to 1d6, or even 0d6) and you lose the ability to Midnight.
Midnight (Crit Success): Two natural sixes. Auto-hits, deals Double Damage. Extra sixes from Advantage increase the effect further.
Snake Eyes (Crit Fail): Two natural ones. Auto-misses with a narrative setback. Cannot occur at 0d6.

12. Attack & Damage Reference

The Weapons section's Dmg and Atk fields are filled in manually — use these formulas to work out what to write in them.

Melee / Finesse / Firearm Attack: 2d6 + Proficiency + Weapon Mod + Accuracy
Magic Attack: 2d6 + Arcane Total + Focus Mod + Accuracy
Spell Damage Bonus: Half your Arcane Total (rounded up), added to every spell you cast

Off-hand attacks use only the base stat score. A weapon with 0 Proficiency is treated as Improvised, using only Might (melee) or Finesse (thrown).

13. Attributes & Skill Totals

At character creation you distribute 3 points across the four attribute groups (Body, Skill, Mind, Style), with a maximum of 2 points in any one group before Species modifiers apply.

Skill Total = Group Base + Skill Mod + Breakpoint Bonus

The Breakpoint Bonus comes from choosing "+1 Attribute Point" instead of a path's passive feature when you unlock a breakpoint on the Paths tab — it only applies to whichever skill you assign it to there.
