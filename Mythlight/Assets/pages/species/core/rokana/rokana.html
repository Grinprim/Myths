Rokana

Source: CORE

Overview

Rokana are small, subterranean humanoids whose bodies carry distinct arthropod features reminiscent of centipedes. Segmented tails or lower bodies, chitinous plating, jointed limbs, and horn- or antenna-like protrusions are common. Their movements are smooth and deliberate, flowing with an unsettling precision well-suited to tunnels, walls, and tight stone passages.

Appearance

Rokana are small, subterranean humanoids whose bodies carry distinct arthropod features reminiscent of centipedes. Segmented tails or lower bodies, chitinous plating, jointed limbs, and horn- or antenna-like protrusions are common. Their movements are smooth and deliberate, flowing with an unsettling precision well-suited to tunnels, walls, and tight stone passages.

Culture

Rokana society is rustic and highly ritualistic, shaped by repetition, tradition, and shared labor. Their homes are carved directly into caverns and fault lines, reinforced with metalwork and stonecraft, and illuminated by carefully maintained light sources. Customs, ceremonies, and daily routines are passed down with little change, providing structure and meaning in the endless dark beneath the surface.
