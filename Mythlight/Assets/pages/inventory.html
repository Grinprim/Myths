Artisan Crafting

Forge items of power using components found in your travels.

Crafting Difficulty & Precision

Crafting requires a Crafting Skill Check (usually Tinkering or an equivalent skill).

Complexity Craft Check
Trivial No check required
Simple Tinkering 8
Standard Tinkering 12
Difficult Tinkering 16
Complex Tinkering 20

Failure by 5 or more: One random component is destroyed.

Precision Component Bonuses

Selecting the correct sub-components grants flat bonuses to the Craft Check.

Precision Bonus
Correct Family Only No bonus
Correct Sub-Component ×1 +1 Craft Bonus
All Components Correct +2 to final roll (global, not per item)

Alchemy & Brewing

Brew potions using aspects, quality, and skill.

Brewing Rules

Brewing Process

During a rest, if you are able to access and use a Capable Brewing Setup, you may attempt to brew a potion of Simple difficulty. Attempting to brew a Medium or Hard recipe requires you to have Brewer as one of your traits.

Brewing consumes all ingredients used in the attempt. All brews require Water (Null) in addition to the ingredients listed in the recipe.

The order of ingredients does not matter. Ingredients may repeat if the recipe calls for it.

Brewing Check

Difficulty Ingredient Slots Target Number
Simple 1 Ingredient ≥ 8
Medium 2 Ingredients ≥ 10
Hard 3 Ingredients ≥ 12

Add the following to your roll:

  • Tinkering Total
  • Brewing Setup Bonus
  • Any other Brewing Bonuses
  • +2 Ingredient Bonus if using multiple ingredients and all are of the same Quality

If the final total is lower than the required value, the attempt fails and you gain nothing. Rolling snake eyes results in a Mystery Brew.

Ingredient Aspects & Codes

Code Aspect Effect
V Vital Regen, HP restoration, Guard gain
C Charge Movement modification, skill enhancement
F Flux Inversion, paradoxical or odd effects
Z Zeal Damage, resistance, offensive scaling
A Arcane Magical effects, mana restoration
N Null (Water) Required stabilizer; used to fill unused recipe slots

Some aspects may be rare in pure form. For example, pure Flux ingredients may be difficult to extract and commonly appear blended with other aspects. Separating aspects from a compound ingredient may require a Tinkering check (Narrator determined difficulty).

Yield & Success

On success, you gain doses equal to the total yield of all ingredients used:

Quality Yield Value
Subpar 0.5 (rounded down; minimum total yield = 1)
Adequate 1
Excellent 2

Add together the yield of all ingredients used in the brew. Minimum total yield is always 1 dose.

Containers

Container Capacity
Vial 1 Dose
Bottle 2 Doses
Jug 3 Doses

A container may only hold one kind of potion. After a successful brew, the batch must be transferred into a container.

Using Potions

  • Drinking or throwing a potion uses an Action.
  • You must spend at least 1 dose to gain its effect.

Recipes & Mystery Brews

Recipes may be granted by traits or discovered in the game world.

  • Brewing a non-Simple potion without knowing its recipe results in a Mystery Brew (except when rolling a Midnight).
  • Mixing two different potions in the same container creates a Mystery Brew.
  • Rolling snake eyes creates a Mystery Brew.
  • A player may poison a batch by mixing brews together, largely negating its effect (Narrator adjudicated).

The effects of a Mystery Brew are determined entirely by the Narrator. They cannot be predicted by Insight or any other roll.
