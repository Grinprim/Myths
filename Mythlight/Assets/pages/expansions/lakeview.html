Lakeview Expansion

Lakeview is a Massive City with multiple layers, and as such has many mechanics not present in the base system. To account for this, this expansion adds a few new traits, paths and systems. The systems bellow are explained from a strictly mechanical perspective, as their utilization in campaigns falls on the narrator to decide how they will be implemented.

Lakeview introduces the Risk Meter, the Smog Meter, two new character paths, the Grafting & Mutation modification system.

Risk Meter

Risk represents bad luck your character has bought themselves through dangerous gambles, morally dubious abilities, and bargains pushed too far. It is tracked on a separate meter and can stack without limit — there is no upper cap, and what eventually happens to a character buried in Risk is left to the Narrator. Risk can be added or removed Narratively, based on the actions your character takes during the course of the campaign. It is usually suggested to keep risk town/city specific

Most Risk is gained voluntarily, by Betting. When a trait lets you Bet, you add Risk to a roll of your choice, but you must declare a success condition before rolling. The more specific the condition, the more Risk you may bet on it:
Bet 1 Risk Must Succeed the Check
Bet 2 Risk Must roll an Odd or Even number
Bet 4 Risk Must roll Above or Below 7
Bet 8 Risk Must roll a specific number
Bet 16 Risk Must roll a specific combination (ex. 3+4 = 7)

Bet Risk is added to your pool upon failing the condition. Beyond betting, several Daredevil and Modified abilities pile on Risk directly: pushing a temporary bonus to its limit, taking a bonus action you weren't entitled to, or forcing a creature to fail a contest can all grant you Risk.

Risk doesn't just sit there. Once it crosses certain thresholds, your body and luck start working against you. These effects are cumulative — at 36 Risk you suffer the Fatigue, no-feat-bonus, and Disadvantage penalties all at once.
12 Risk You suffer 1 point of Fatigue for as long as your Risk remains at or above this threshold.
24 Risk You no longer gain Feat bonuses on rolls.
36 Risk You suffer constant Disadvantage on all rolls.
48 Risk You no longer have Final Blow Resistances.

Risk at or above these thresholds is dangerous specifically because it's so easy to keep stacking — nothing stops you from betting again while already Fatigued, and a character at 48+ Risk is one unlucky Final Blow away from death with nothing left to resist it.

New Paths

Lakeview adds two optional paths built around its two new resources: the Path of the Daredevil (the "Risky Fella"), who gambles with Risk, and the Path of the Modified, who changes their body beyond mortal limits.

Path of the Daredevil

Revolves entirely around the Risk mechanic: you gamble certainty for power, betting on rolls for outsized bonuses. Fortune becomes a resource you constantly spend and constantly risk losing.

  • Breakpoint 3: Spend all of your Grace to remove that much Risk.
  • Breakpoint 6: When you roll with a bet, you may shift a rolled die value by +1/-1.

Path of the Modified

Built around replacing and enhancing the body through Grafts and Mutations. The character becomes a modular construct of biological, mechanical, and arcane modification, gaining power through sacrifice while becoming more dependent on alterations and less resilient naturally.

  • Breakpoint 3: Maintain 1 additional active Modification beyond your normal maximum.
  • Breakpoint 6: Install or remove a Graft during a Rest without needing surgical tools or a specialized facility. You gain advantage on checks revolving around instilation of modifications or stabilization of mutations.

Smog

Smog is your bodies reaction to the toxic fumes of the lower levels, as smog builds up in your body, it imposes negative side effects, that will make you much weaker than when you came to lake view. Removing smog is not neccesarily difficult, as you just need fresh air or a gas mask etc.

Smog is tracked on its own meter, capped at 12. Smog doesn't impose a status effect, it eats directly into your Health pool by capping how much of your maximum Health you can actually use.
6 Smog Your maximum Health is halved.
9 Smog Your maximum Health is reduced to a third.
12 Smog Your maximum Health is reduced to a quarter.

These thresholds don't stack with each other — only the highest one you've reached applies. If your current Health exceeds your new (reduced) maximum, it drops to match. A Modified character running several active Grafts and Mutations at once should expect to be living with a permanently smaller Health pool in exchange for everything those modifications are doing for them.

Grafting & Mutation

Grafting is the process of permanently replacing or augmenting a part of your body with a built component — mechanical, bone, arcane, or otherwise — in exchange for a permanent benefit. Mutation works the same way through biological alteration instead of surgical replacement (injected or consumed mutagens rather than installed parts). In exchange for not requiring physical components, Mutations are inherently unstable and must be kept in check — usually with medication, as detailed below.

Installing a Modification: Normally, installing or removing a Graft requires external intervention — a Graft shop, or Wild Mutagenic Material for a Mutation. Certain traits let you perform the Grafting or Mutating yourself instead. Either way, a Graft still needs its materials supplied for the procedure to succeed, and a Mutation still needs its proper medication on hand so it doesn't consume you.

Modification Slots

You may only maintain a limited number of active Modifications (Grafts and Mutations, combined) at once. This maximum scales with Level, and Path of the Modified traits can push it higher still:

Level 1-3 1 active Modification
Level 4-6 2 active Modifications
Level 7-9 (Trait Required) 3 active Modifications
Level 10-12 (Trait Required) 4 active Modifications

A destroyed Graft is unusable until repaired or replaced. A Mutation has no physical part to break, so it can only be removed during a Rest instead.

Mutation Stability

Every active Mutation must be dosed with its Stabilizing Medication once per Rest. This is what you're paying instead of a material cost — skip a dose and the Mutation starts metabolizing you instead.

For each active Mutation left unmedicated through a Rest, gain Smog equal to that Mutation's Rank. An unmedicated Mutation's beneficial effect still functions — you're not losing the upside, only paying for it through Smog instead of medication.

Stabilizing Medication is consumed on use and must be restocked like any other supply, typically from an apothecary, Graft shop, or specialist willing to work with Wild Mutagenic Material. A character who can't reliably afford or access their medication should expect their Smog, and the Health penalty that comes with it, to creep upward over time.

Stabalizing Medication can also be crafted, using [Vital][Vital][Flux][Arcane] you may use a Brewing Kit capable of creating Hard potions to do so, as long as you have the abilty to use such a tool.

Upgrading a Graft

Most Grafts and Mutations can be taken more than once, with each additional copy representing a higher-quality or more deeply integrated version rather than a second, separate part. Installing Rank 2 of a Graft you already have replaces Rank 1 in the same slot, costs the full material price again, and increases its effect — usually by simply scaling the bonus, though some Grafts unlock a new clause at higher ranks instead. A Graft's maximum rank is set per-Graft.
